Mofongo Recipe

A Puerto Rican comfort dish

Mofongo is a dish that is made using green plantains and is a common dish in Puerto Rican cuisine. It was never made often in my house, but when it was it never lasted.

Mofongo is made from fried green plantains that is the mashed together with a garlic paste and bits of crispy pork, usually served in a pilon, a wooden mortar. Now there are many different ways to make mofongo and I prefer my mofongo to be made with a sauce that can be poured over the mashed plantains.

Ingredients

  • Vegetable oil, for frying
  • 3 plaintains(green,unripe)
  • 1 cup pork cracklings, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste

Steps

  1. Heat about 2 inches of oil over medium-high heat in a large skillet or deep fryer to 350
  2. While the oil is heating up, peel the plantains and cut into 1-inch rounds.
  3. Fry the plantains until golden and tender, 4 to 6 min.
  4. Remove cooked plantains from the pan or fryer to a paper towel lined plate
  5. Put the garlic paste in a large bowl or mortar and add the garlic plantains, in batches, if necessary. Mash thoroughly.
  6. Add the pork rinds. Continue to mash and mix until all of the ingredients are combined. Add salt to taste.
  7. Shape the mofongo into 4 balls and serve.
